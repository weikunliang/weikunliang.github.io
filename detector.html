Problem

Carbon monoxide is a colorless, odorless and tasteless gas and can cause illnesses from headache and nausea to death. According to research, every year, 20,000 to 30,000 people in the US are sickened by accidental carbon monoxide poisoning and approximately 500 people die, many in their own home.


Existing Systems

We did some research on existing carbon monoxide detectors. We found out that most carbon monoxide detectors only come in one and users usually install it in the basement where it is not as effective. Another problem is many popular CO detectors usually have the amount of CO in the room on the display, but this number is usually meaningless to the user, since the user wouldn’t know how much CO is considered deathly. In addition, the battery low indicator is often meaningless too since it just gives off a weak light, and is often unnoticed by the user.


Persona



Laura is a middle school teacher in her late 30’s. She lives in Michigan with her husband and 3 children in a 3 storey house. Their house includes a fireplace where they often use because of the cold weather in Michigan. The fireplace saves Laura money on home-heating bills.
